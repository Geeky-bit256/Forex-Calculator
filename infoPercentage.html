Percentage Clarification

Calculating Percentage

Deposit Profit made Percentage
R1,000 R10,000 900%
R1,000 R100,000 9,000%
R1,000 R100,000 99,000%

When calculating the percentage of profit you've made, it's essential to exclude your initial deposit from the calculation. This ensures that you're measuring the return on your investment accurately.

For instance, let's say you deposit R1,000 and you've made a profit of R10,000. To calculate the percentage return, you would divide the profit (R10,000) by the deposit (R1,000), which gives you a return of 900%. This means that your investment has grown by 900% of its original value.

It's worth noting that if you include your initial deposit in the calculation, the percentage return would be 1,000%. However, this would be misleading, as it would imply that your investment has grown by 1,000%of its original value, rather than 900%.

By excluding your deposit from the calculation, you get a more accurate picture of your investment's performance and can make informed decisions about future investments.
